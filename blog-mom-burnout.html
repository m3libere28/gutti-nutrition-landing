The Science of Stress & Gut Health

The connection between our gut and our brain is real and powerful. This is known as the Gut-Brain Axis.

[Infographic: The Cycle of Stress & Gut Health]
Visualizing how stress alters gut bacteria -> increases inflammation -> causes fatigue.

Research has shown that the microbiome plays a significant role in influencing anxiety and depression (Foster & Neufeld, 2013). Furthermore, chronic stress can directly alter our gut microbiota, creating a cycle that affects our mood and energy levels (Madison & Kiecolt-Glaser, 2019).

3 Steps to Reclaim Your Energy

  1. Prioritize "Rest & Digest": Take 3 deep breaths before eating. It sounds simple, but it switches your nervous system from fight-or-flight to digestion mode.
  2. Add Prebiotics, Not Just Probiotics: Feed your good bacteria with foods like bananas, oats, and asparagus.
  3. Hydrate with Minerals: Plain water isn't enough when you're stressed. Add a pinch of sea salt or lemon.
