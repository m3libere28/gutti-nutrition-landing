1. Beyond Calories: The Hidden Drivers

We cannot ignore the "invisible" factors that tell your body to store fat.

  • Cortisol (Stress): High stress signals your body to hold onto weight around the midsection as a survival mechanism.
  • Sleep Quality: Poor sleep disrupts your hunger hormones (ghrelin and leptin), making you crave sugar the next day.
  • Nutrient Timing: It's not just what you eat, but when. Eating in sync with your circadian rhythm changes how your body processes energy.

2. The Science of the "Set Point"

Think of your metabolism like a thermostat. When you crash diet, your body thinks you are starving. It reacts by slowing down your metabolism to "save" energy. This is why you rebound so quickly after a diet ends.
